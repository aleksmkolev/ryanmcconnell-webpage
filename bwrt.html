Brain Working Recursive Therapy (BWRT)

Revolutionary therapy targeting the subconscious mind for rapid emotional healing

BWRT is a cutting-edge therapeutic approach that works with the subconscious mind to create rapid and lasting change. This innovative therapy involves altering negative memories and emotional responses at their source.

How BWRT Works

This therapy involves altering the subconscious mind. It takes a negative memory (of any given emotion) and overlays a preferred response (usually a calm and happy memory) with a special technique. After the process is concluded the negative emotion associated with initial memory gets removed, leaving the client feeling apathetic towards the memory.

BWRT works before the mind has a chance to process information consciously, making it incredibly effective for trauma, phobias, and deeply rooted emotional patterns.

What BWRT Can Treat

Conditions Treated with BWRT

  • Trauma & PTSD
  • Phobias & Fears
  • Anxiety Disorders
  • Sleep Disorders
  • Relationship Issues
  • Unwanted Habits
  • Depression
  • Performance Issues

The BWRT Process

1

Identify the Target

We identify the specific memory, emotion, or response pattern that needs to be addressed.

2

Create Preferred Response

Together we develop the ideal emotional and behavioral response you'd prefer to have.

3

Subconscious Reprocessing

Using BWRT techniques, we overlay the new preferred response onto the existing neural pathway.

4

Integration & Testing

We test the new response and ensure it's fully integrated into your subconscious patterns.

Why BWRT is Unique

Unlike traditional therapies that work with conscious thought processes, BWRT targets the brain's rapid response system before conscious awareness kicks in. This makes it exceptionally effective for automatic emotional and behavioral responses that seem beyond conscious control.

Ready to Experience BWRT?

BWRT often produces results in just a few sessions, making it an efficient choice for addressing deep-rooted emotional patterns and trauma.
